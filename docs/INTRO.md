# HackNation Submission Overview

## Challenge

**Maschmeyer Group - The VC Brain** asks teams to reimagine venture investing at
100x speed and enable an evidence-backed decision on a $100,000 investment
within 24 hours.

## Product Thesis

Speed alone is not the hard part. An LLM can summarize a deck quickly, but a
venture decision still fails when claims lose their sources, contradictions are
missed, sparse founder data is treated as negative evidence, or the final memo
cannot explain why confidence changed.

Iskra treats diligence as a living evidence graph. It stores sources, claims,
evidence, founder history, score snapshots, and investor context separately,
then lets the intelligence layer reason over those records. The result is fast,
traceable, and designed for collaborative investment work.

## Core Workflow

1. Add a company from Iskra chat or the analysis modal.
2. Upload a deck, founder document, market note, spreadsheet, or customer
   reference.
3. Pull selected public sources and normalize them into the same schema.
4. Extract multiple typed claims per source and link each claim to evidence.
5. Resolve support, disputes, missing evidence, and founder cold-start status.
6. Evaluate thesis fit and score Founder, Market, and Idea-vs-Market
   independently.
7. Generate an evidence-linked memo, red-team view, decision, and decision-flip
   conditions.
8. Assign diligence tasks, leave contextual comments, simulate outcomes, and
   continue the review through Iskra text or voice.

## Differentiators

### Evidence before eloquence

The system records confidence reasons, evidence links, source independence,
freshness, and directness. Model-generated prose cannot silently replace the
underlying record.

### Founder memory across deals

Founder Scores and Passports persist beyond one application. Employment,
education, previous ventures, outcomes, skills, and public profiles remain
source-linked and deduplicated.

### Honest cold starts

Sparse data lowers confidence rather than automatically lowering founder
quality. Iskra makes the gap explicit and recommends the next evidence action.

### Causal decisions

The Decision Flight Recorder shows how a new source changed a claim, score,
readiness level, or trigger event. Decision-flip conditions explain what must
be verified for the recommendation to change.

### Voice as an operating surface

Dictation fills the normal chat composer. Dialogue mode transcribes, reasons,
speaks, and resumes listening. Both modes can search the portfolio, answer
evidence-grounded questions, or prepare a new analysis.

## Demo Narrative

The recommended presentation follows one company through intake, founder
memory, independent evidence, a seeded contradiction, three-axis scoring, and
the investment memo. A cold-start company demonstrates uncertainty handling;
natural-language sourcing and voice show the same data model working across
inbound and outbound workflows.

## Responsible Framing

Iskra supports an investor; it does not replace one. Scores are decision aids,
not objective truth. The system exposes missing data and disputed evidence,
keeps the three investment axes separate, and labels synthetic demo material
clearly.
